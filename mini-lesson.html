What is Phishing?

Phishing is when someone pretends to be a trusted person or service to get you to click a link, sign in, or share information.

The goal is often to:

  • capture login credentials
  • access email or files
  • send messages as you

Where Phishing Appears at Drexel

Phishing does not only happen in email. It can appear in:

  • Outlook messages
  • Microsoft Teams chats
  • shared files and SharePoint links
💡 If you can click on Microsoft 365, it can be imitated.

The 10-Second Check

Before clicking:

  • Check the sender, look beyond the display name
  • Check the request, urgency and pressure are common tactics
  • Check the link or file, look at where it leads before opening
  • Check the context, does this match your workflow?

Safe Actions

  • Verify using a trusted contact
  • Report the message
  • Avoid clicking until confirming
💡 If you already clicked: close it and report immediately.
